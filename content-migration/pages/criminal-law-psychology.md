## Types of Criminal Law Assessments We Provide

### Fitness to Plead (Pritchard Criteria)
Comprehensive assessment against the five Pritchard criteria:
1. Understand the charge
2. Understand the plea
3. Instruct solicitors
4. Challenge jurors
5. Follow evidence

Report addresses whether defendant meets criteria and, if not, prospects for remediation.

### Fitness to Participate (Effective Participation)
Assessment of defendant's capacity to participate effectively in trial, including:
- Ability to give evidence
- Capacity to understand proceedings
- Ability to make decisions about plea and trial strategy
- Communication difficulties and reasonable adjustments needed

### Diminished Responsibility (s.2 Homicide Act)
Specialist assessment of whether defendant's mental functioning was substantially impaired at time of killing, covering:
- Abnormality of mental functioning
- Recognized medical condition
- Substantial impairment of ability to understand conduct, form rational judgment, or exercise self-control
- Causal link between condition and killing

### Insanity Defense (M'Naghten Rules)
Assessment of defendant's mental state at time of offense to establish:
- Defect of reason from disease of the mind
- Inability to know nature and quality of act, or
- Inability to know act was wrong

### Criminal Responsibility & Intent
Psychological assessment of defendant's capacity to form mens rea, particularly relevant for:
- Learning disability affecting understanding
- Mental illness affecting judgment
- Neurological impairment affecting impulse control

### Pre-Sentencing Psychological Report
Comprehensive psychological context for sentencing, including:
- Mental health history and current presentation
- Cognitive functioning and learning disability
- Risk assessment and protective factors
- Treatability and rehabilitation prospects
- Sentencing recommendations

### Risk Assessment (Reoffending)
Structured risk assessment using validated tools (HCR-20, SARA, SVR-20) addressing:
- Risk of violent reoffending
- Risk of sexual reoffending (where relevant)
- Risk factors and protective factors
- Risk management recommendations

### Suggestibility & False Confession
Specialist assessment of vulnerability to suggestion in police interview, using:
- Gudjonsson Suggestibility Scales (GSS)
- IQ and cognitive testing
- Mental health assessment
- Analysis of interview evidence

### Witness Reliability Assessment
Psychological evaluation of witness capacity to give accurate evidence, considering:
- Cognitive function and memory
- Suggestibility and compliance
- Mental health factors affecting reliability
- Communication difficulties

### Neuropsychological Assessment
Specialist assessment of brain injury or neurological impairment, including:
- Traumatic brain injury (TBI)
- Acquired brain injury (stroke, hypoxia)
- Neurodegenerative conditions (dementia)
- Impact on behavior, impulse control, and judgment

## FAQs

**Q: What is the difference between fitness to plead and fitness to participate?**
A: **Fitness to plead** is the threshold question of whether defendant can engage in trial at all (Pritchard criteria). **Fitness to participate** assumes defendant is fit to plead but asks whether reasonable adjustments are needed for effective participation (e.g., intermediary, frequent breaks). Our psychologists assess both.

**Q: Can your psychologists assess defendants in custody?**
A: Yes. Our experts are experienced in prison-based assessments and hold enhanced DBS clearance. They are familiar with security protocols and can conduct assessments in police custody, remand prisons, or secure hospitals.

**Q: Do you work at Legal Aid rates for criminal cases?**
A: Yes. Our experts accept Legal Aid Agency (LAA) rates for both defense and prosecution work. We can provide transparent fixed-fee quotes within 24 hours of instruction.

**Q: What are typical turnaround times for fitness to plead assessments?**
A: Standard fitness to plead reports are delivered within **3-4 weeks** from instruction. **Fast-track options** are available for urgent cases â€” we can deliver comprehensive assessments in 7-10 days when required (e.g., approaching custody time limits).

**Q: Are your experts qualified to give evidence in Crown Court?**
A: Yes. All our experts are HCPC-registered, professionally insured, and experienced in giving oral evidence in Crown Court. They understand cross-examination and how to present complex psychological evidence to juries and judges.

**Q: What tools and tests do your psychologists use?**
A: Our experts use validated, evidence-based tools including WAIS-IV (IQ testing), Gudjonsson Suggestibility Scales (suggestibility), HCR-20 (violence risk), comprehensive neuropsychological batteries, and clinical diagnostic interviews. All testing is referenced in reports.
