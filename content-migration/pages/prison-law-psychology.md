## FAQs

**Q: Do your psychologists visit prisons in person?**
A: Yes. Our experts are experienced in secure environments and can conduct in-person assessments at prisons nationwide. They hold enhanced DBS certificates and are familiar with security protocols. Remote assessments may also be possible depending on the case type and prison facility.

**Q: Can you provide reports under Legal Aid funding?**
A: Absolutely. Many of our experts work at Legal Aid Agency (LAA) rates, and we can advise on eligibility and pricing during your initial enquiry.

**Q: What are typical turnaround times for prison law reports?**
A: Standard reports are delivered within **4-6 weeks** from instruction. **Fast-track options** are available for urgent cases such as imminent parole hearings or recall reviews â€” we can deliver comprehensive reports in 1-2 weeks when required.

**Q: Are your experts qualified to give evidence in court or parole hearings?**
A: Yes. All experts are HCPC-registered, professionally insured, and experienced in providing oral evidence in court, tribunal, and parole board hearings. Many have given evidence hundreds of times.

**Q: What risk assessment tools do your psychologists use?**
A: Our experts use validated, evidence-based tools including HCR-20 (violence risk), SARA (spousal assault risk), RSVP/SVR-20 (sexual violence risk), and PCL-R (psychopathy), alongside clinical formulation and professional judgment.

**Q: Can you provide assessments for prisoners with language barriers?**
A: Yes. We can arrange interpreters or, where possible, match you with psychologists who speak the required language.
