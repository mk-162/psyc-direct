## What Are Cookies?

A cookie is a small text file stored on your computer or device when you visit a website. It helps websites remember your preferences, track how you use them, and personalise your experience.

Cookies aren't harmful -- they can't access your files or personal information. But they do collect data about how you browse, so we think you should know what we're using and why.

## Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

- **Keep the website working** -- so you can log in, stay logged in, and use site features without issues
- **Understand how you use our site** -- which pages help you most, where visitors drop off, what content works
- **Show you relevant content** -- personalising your experience based on your interests
- **Measure marketing effectiveness** -- so we know which campaigns reach the right people

## Categories of Cookies We Use

### Essential Cookies (always active)

These keep the website running and can't be disabled. They include:

- Session cookies (remember that you're logged in)
- Security cookies (protect against fraud)
- Preference cookies (remember your language or region)

### Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with the site (which pages, how long they stay, where they come from). This helps us improve content and user experience.

These cookies are optional. You can turn them off below without affecting site function, though we won't be able to see what's working.

### Marketing Cookies

We use Facebook and LinkedIn to show you relevant ads about our services if you've visited the site. These cookies track whether you've seen or clicked on ads.

You can turn off marketing cookies if you prefer not to see targeted ads.

### Third-Party Cookies

Third parties (like Google, Facebook, LinkedIn) may set their own cookies when you visit our site. Refer to their policies for details on how they use data.

## Managing Your Cookies

**On this website:** Use the cookie preference centre at the bottom of the page to enable or disable Analytics and Marketing cookies. Essential cookies always stay active.

**In your browser:** Most browsers let you control cookies in settings. You can block new cookies or delete existing ones. Note: blocking cookies may affect site function or our ability to recognise you.

**Opt out of targeted ads:** Visit Google's ads preferences page or the Network Advertising Initiative to control ad targeting across the web.

## How Long We Keep Cookies

Essential cookies last only as long as your session (closed when you leave the site). Analytics and marketing cookies typically expire after 12-24 months, though some may persist longer.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update our cookie usage to reflect new tools or legal requirements. We'll notify you of material changes and ask for re-consent if needed.
