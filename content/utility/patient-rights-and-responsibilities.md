## Your Rights

### Right to Respectful Care
You have the right to:
- Receive considerate, respectful, and compassionate care at all times
- Be treated with dignity, regardless of your age, gender, race, religion, sexual orientation, or disability
- Receive care in a safe, clean, and comfortable environment
- Have your cultural, spiritual, and personal values respected

### Right to Information
You have the right to:
- Receive clear, honest information about your health status and prognosis
- Understand your diagnosis, treatment options, and potential outcomes
- Ask questions and receive answers you can understand
- Access your medical records in accordance with data protection laws
- Receive information about the qualifications and experience of your care team

### Right to Informed Consent
You have the right to:
- Be fully informed before any treatment, procedure, or service
- Understand the benefits, risks, and alternatives to proposed care
- Give or withhold consent for any treatment
- Change your mind and withdraw consent at any time
- Refuse treatment, understanding the potential consequences

### Right to Privacy & Confidentiality
You have the right to:
- Have your personal and medical information kept confidential
- Expect that your records will only be accessed by authorised personnel
- Control how your information is shared, in accordance with the law
- Request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of your information

### Right to Quality Care
You have the right to:
- Receive care that meets professional standards of quality
- Have your pain assessed and managed appropriately
- Receive a timely response to your healthcare needs
- Expect continuity of care and clear communication between providers
- Receive appropriate referrals when specialised care is needed

### Right to Make Complaints & Provide Feedback
You have the right to:
- Voice concerns or complaints without fear of retaliation
- Have complaints investigated promptly and fairly
- Receive a response to your complaint within a reasonable timeframe
- Escalate complaints to external bodies if dissatisfied with our response
- Provide feedback that helps us improve our services

### Right to Choose
You have the right to:
- Choose your care providers within the constraints of your membership
- Seek a second opinion
- Participate in decisions about your care
- Accept or refuse participation in research or clinical trials

## Your Responsibilities

### Providing Information
To help us care for you effectively, we ask that you:
- Provide accurate and complete information about your health history
- Tell us about all medications, supplements, and treatments you're using
- Inform us of any changes in your health status
- Share information about allergies and adverse reactions
- Notify us of any hospitalisations or emergency care received elsewhere

### Following the Care Plan
We ask that you:
- Follow the treatment plan we develop together
- Take medications as prescribed and report any side effects
- Attend scheduled appointments or provide adequate notice if you need to cancel
- Inform us if you don't understand or cannot follow your care plan
- Participate actively in your wellness journey

### Respecting Others
We expect that you will:
- Treat our staff and other members with courtesy and respect
- Respect the privacy and comfort of other members
- Follow our policies regarding appointment times and cancellations
- Use our facilities and equipment responsibly
- Not engage in abusive, threatening, or violent behaviour

### Financial Responsibilities
We ask that you:
- Understand and fulfil your financial obligations as outlined in your membership agreement
- Provide accurate insurance information when applicable
- Pay for services not covered by your membership or insurance
- Inform us promptly of any changes to your insurance status

### Communication
We encourage you to:
- Ask questions when you don't understand something
- Express your concerns, preferences, and needs clearly
- Inform us if you're not satisfied with your care
- Participate in shared decision-making about your health

## Making a Complaint

If you have concerns about your care, we want to hear from you. Our complaints process is designed to be fair, transparent, and responsive.

### Step 1: Informal Resolution
Speak directly with the staff member involved or ask to speak with a supervisor. Many concerns can be resolved quickly through open communication.

### Step 2: Formal Complaint
If informal resolution is not satisfactory, you may submit a formal complaint:
- **By Email**: complaints@cocoonwellness.com
- **By Post**: Complaints Manager, Cocoon Wellness, [Address]
- **In Person**: Request to speak with our Member Experience Director

We will acknowledge your complaint within 2 working days and provide a full response within 20 working days.

### Step 3: External Review
If you're not satisfied with our response, you may escalate your complaint to:
- **Care Quality Commission (CQC)**: www.cqc.org.uk
- **Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman**: www.ombudsman.org.uk
