## Introduction

Psychology Direct Limited ("we" or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how long we keep it, and your rights under UK data protection law.

We're the data controller for any information you provide through this website or when you contact us directly. If you have questions about this policy or your data, our Data Protection Officer is always available to help.

## What Data We Collect

We collect information to understand your needs and provide the best possible service:

- **Your contact details** -- name, email, phone, organisation
- **Case information** -- only what's relevant to your instruction (case type, urgency, subject matter)
- **Technical data** -- browser type, device, pages visited, to help us improve the site
- **Preferences** -- how you'd like us to communicate with you

We never ask for sensitive data (health, race, religion) unless it's directly relevant to your case.

## Why We Use Your Data

We use your information to:

- Match you with the right expert psychologist or psychiatrist
- Keep you updated about your case or enquiry
- Improve our website and service quality
- Respond to legal requests from courts or regulators
- Comply with our legal and professional obligations

## How Long We Keep Your Data

We keep your data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose it was collected for. For case files, we typically retain records for 7 years after the case closes (a legal requirement). Contact details are held until you ask us to delete them.

## Your Rights Under GDPR

You have the right to:

- **Access** -- request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
- **Correct** -- ask us to update information that's inaccurate or incomplete
- **Delete** -- request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
- **Restrict** -- limit how we use your information
- **Portability** -- receive your data in a structured, portable format
- **Object** -- opt out of marketing or certain types of processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at enquiries@psychologydirect.co.uk or call 01306 879 975.

## Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses cookies to improve your experience (see our [Cookies Policy](/cookie-policy/) for details). These help us understand which pages are useful and let us tailor content to your needs. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but some site features may not work as well.

## Third Parties & Data Sharing

We never sell, share, or distribute your personal data to third parties without your permission. The only exceptions are:

- When legally required (court order, regulator request, safeguarding concern)
- To the psychologist or psychiatrist matched to your case (only the information they need)
- To our trusted service providers (e.g., secure email hosting) who sign data-processing agreements

## Security

We use physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards to protect your data. Our systems are regularly reviewed and updated. However, no internet transmission is 100% secure -- please use your own judgment when sharing sensitive information online.

## Links to Other Sites

Our website may link to other organisations' sites. We're not responsible for their privacy practices. We recommend reviewing their privacy policies before sharing your data.
