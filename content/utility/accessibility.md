## Our Commitment

Psychology Direct is committed to ensuring our website is accessible to everyone, regardless of ability or disability. We believe that online access is a fundamental right and we're working continuously to improve our site's accessibility.

We aim to meet or exceed the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standard, which is the UK legal requirement for public sector organisations and best practice for private businesses.

## What WCAG 2.1 AA Means

WCAG 2.1 AA is an international standard for accessible web design. It ensures:

- **Perceivable content** -- text is readable, images have descriptions, videos have captions
- **Operable design** -- the site works with keyboard only (no mouse needed) and with screen readers
- **Understandable structure** -- pages are clear, headings are logical, language is plain
- **Robust code** -- the site works with assistive technologies and different browsers

Our website meets these standards across most pages.

## Accessibility Features We've Implemented

- **Screen reader compatible** -- our site works with NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver
- **Keyboard navigation** -- use Tab to navigate, Enter to select, Esc to close
- **Text resizing** -- browser zoom works (Ctrl/Cmd + or -)
- **Colour contrast** -- text is readable against backgrounds (minimum 4.5:1 ratio)
- **Descriptive links** -- link text clearly explains where it goes
- **Image descriptions** -- alt text describes what images show
- **Video captions** -- any videos include captions and transcripts
- **Skip links** -- jump directly to main content without reading navigation
- **Focus indicators** -- clear visible focus when using keyboard

## Known Limitations

We aim for full accessibility, but some older pages or third-party tools may have barriers:

- Some PDF documents may not be fully tagged for screen readers (we're working to fix this)
- Contact forms require JavaScript (alternative contact methods are available below)
- Some embedded videos from third parties may lack captions

If you encounter any of these issues, please let us know and we'll help or provide an alternative.

## Accessibility & Data Protection

When you report an accessibility issue, we may collect personal information (name, email, phone) to respond to you. This is handled under our [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy/). We never share accessibility reports with third parties without your consent.

## Third-Party Tools & Links

Our website may include third-party tools (payments, forms, maps) and external links. While we choose accessible vendors, we're not responsible for their accessibility. If you have trouble using a linked service, please let us know and we'll try to help.

## Legal Basis for Accessibility

We maintain our accessibility commitment to comply with:

- **Equality Act 2010** (UK law requiring reasonable adjustments for disabled people)
- **Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018** (for any public sector work we do)
- **WCAG 2.1 Level AA** (international best practice standard)
