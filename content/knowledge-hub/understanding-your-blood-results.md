## Why Understanding Your Results Matters

You've had a health screening — excellent. But a sheet of numbers only helps if you know what they mean. This guide walks you through the key markers we test at Cocoon and what your results are telling you.

## The Basics: Full Blood Count

Your full blood count (FBC) is the foundation of any screening. It measures:

- **Haemoglobin** — carries oxygen in your blood. Low levels suggest anaemia; high levels may indicate dehydration or other conditions.
- **White blood cells** — your immune system's front line. Elevated counts can signal infection or inflammation.
- **Platelets** — essential for clotting. Abnormal levels warrant further investigation.

## Metabolic Markers

These reveal how well your body processes energy:

- **HbA1c** — your average blood sugar over 3 months. Below 42 mmol/mol is normal; 42–47 is pre-diabetic; above 48 indicates diabetes.
- **Fasting glucose** — a snapshot of current blood sugar levels.
- **Insulin** — high fasting insulin (even with normal glucose) is an early warning sign of metabolic dysfunction.

## Liver Function

Your liver is remarkably resilient — but it's also remarkably quiet when things go wrong:

- **ALT and AST** — liver enzymes. Elevated levels suggest inflammation or damage.
- **GGT** — particularly sensitive to alcohol and medication effects.
- **Albumin** — a marker of your liver's synthetic function and overall nutritional status.

## Hormonal Panel

Hormones govern energy, mood, weight, and cognition:

- **Thyroid (TSH, FT4, FT3)** — an underactive thyroid is one of the most common and most missed causes of fatigue and weight gain.
- **Testosterone** — relevant for both men and women. Declining levels affect energy, muscle mass, mood, and libido.
- **Cortisol** — your stress hormone. Chronically elevated cortisol disrupts sleep, immunity, and metabolism.
- **Vitamin D** — technically a hormone, and deficient in the majority of UK adults. Essential for bones, immunity, and mood.

## Cardiovascular Risk

- **Total cholesterol** — less useful alone. The ratio matters more.
- **LDL** — "bad" cholesterol that builds up in arteries.
- **HDL** — "good" cholesterol that helps remove LDL.
- **Triglycerides** — elevated by sugar, alcohol, and excess carbohydrates.
- **ApoB** — increasingly recognised as the single best predictor of cardiovascular risk.

## What "Normal" Really Means

A result within the reference range doesn't necessarily mean *optimal*. Reference ranges are based on population averages — including people who are unwell. At Cocoon, we look at where you sit within the range and track trends over time.

A TSH of 4.0 is technically "normal" but may be suboptimal if your previous reading was 1.5. Context matters.

## When to Retest

We typically recommend:

- **Annual screening** from age 40 (or earlier with family history)
- **6-month follow-up** if any markers were borderline
- **3-month recheck** after starting new treatment or lifestyle changes
