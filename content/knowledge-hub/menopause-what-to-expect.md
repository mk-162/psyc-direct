## A Different Conversation About Menopause

Menopause isn't a condition to be endured. It's a hormonal transition that, with the right support, can be managed proactively — preserving your energy, clarity, confidence, and long-term health.

At Cocoon, we take menopause seriously. Not as an afterthought, but as a core part of women's healthcare.

## When Does It Start?

Most women enter perimenopause in their mid-40s, though it can begin as early as the late 30s. The average age of menopause (defined as 12 months without a period) in the UK is 51.

**Perimenopause** is the transition phase — and it's often the hardest part. Hormone levels fluctuate unpredictably, producing symptoms that can be confusing and disruptive:

- Hot flushes and night sweats
- Sleep disruption
- Brain fog and difficulty concentrating
- Mood changes, anxiety, low mood
- Joint pain and muscle aches
- Weight gain (particularly around the middle)
- Reduced libido
- Heart palpitations

## The Testing Question

A common misconception: "A blood test will tell me if I'm menopausal." In reality, hormone levels fluctuate so much during perimenopause that a single snapshot is often unreliable.

What testing *can* do:

- **Confirm menopause** in women under 45 (FSH levels)
- **Baseline your hormones** before starting HRT
- **Rule out other causes** — thyroid dysfunction, vitamin deficiencies, and anaemia can mimic menopause symptoms
- **Monitor treatment** — ensure your HRT dosing is optimal

## Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT)

HRT replaces the hormones your body is no longer producing in sufficient quantities. Modern HRT is:

- **Safe for most women** when started within 10 years of menopause
- **Available in multiple forms** — patches, gels, sprays, tablets
- **Tailored to you** — oestrogen-only, combined, or testosterone addition depending on symptoms
- **Protective** — reduces risk of osteoporosis, cardiovascular disease, and possibly dementia

The risks are lower than many believe. The 2002 WHI study that frightened a generation has been extensively re-analysed. For women starting HRT under 60, the benefits overwhelmingly outweigh the risks.

## Beyond HRT

Hormones are part of the picture, but not all of it. Our menopause support also covers:

- **Nutrition** — dietary changes that support hormonal balance, bone density, and cardiovascular health
- **Exercise** — strength training becomes especially important for maintaining muscle mass and bone health
- **Sleep hygiene** — practical strategies for managing disrupted sleep
- **Supplements** — evidence-based options for specific symptoms
- **Mental health** — CBT and mindfulness approaches for mood and anxiety
