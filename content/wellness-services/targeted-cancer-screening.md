# Screening Tailored to You

While general cancer screening is valuable, some individuals benefit from more targeted approaches based on family history, genetic factors, lifestyle, or specific concerns. Our Targeted Cancer Screening provides personalised protocols designed around your unique risk profile.

| When Targeted Screening Helps | Our Approach |
|------------------------------|--------------|
| **Family History** | Enhanced screening for relevant cancers |
| **Genetic Mutations** | BRCA, Lynch syndrome, and others |
| **Lifestyle Factors** | Smoking, occupational exposures |
| **Previous Cancer** | Surveillance for recurrence or new primaries |
| **Specific Concerns** | Focused investigation of symptoms |

# Risk Assessment

## Family History Review

| Detail | What We Assess |
|--------|----------------|
| **Affected Relatives** | Who had cancer, what type, age at diagnosis |

| **Genetic Risk** | Likelihood of inherited predisposition |

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|

| **Lifestyle** | Smoking, alcohol, diet, exercise |

| **Medications** | Some affect cancer risk |

# Screening Options

## Breast Cancer (High Risk)

| Screening | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Mammography** | Annually from age 40 (or earlier if indicated) |
| **MRI** | Annually for very high risk |
| **Clinical Examination** | Every 6-12 months |

## Colorectal Cancer (High Risk)

| Screening | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Colonoscopy** | Every 1-5 years depending on risk |
| **FIT Test** | Annually if colonoscopy not indicated |

## Prostate Cancer (High Risk)

| Screening | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **PSA Testing** | Annually from age 45 (or 40 for very high risk) |
| **Digital Examination** | Annually |

## Lung Cancer (High Risk)

| Screening | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Low-Dose CT** | Annually for high-risk smokers/ex-smokers |

## Other Cancers

| Cancer Type | Screening Approach |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Ovarian** | CA-125 + ultrasound for high risk |
| **Pancreatic** | MRI/MRCP for very high risk |
| **Skin** | Dermatology examination |

# Genetic Testing

## When Considered

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Strong Family History** | Multiple relatives with same/related cancers |
| **Early-Onset Cancer** | Cancer in family member <50 years |
| **Rare Cancers** | Ovarian, male breast cancer |
| **Multiple Primary Cancers** | In same individual |

| Panel | Cancers Covered |

# Your Questions Answered

**Will insurance cover genetic testing?**

Some insurers cover genetic testing for high-risk individuals. We can provide documentation to support insurance claims.

**What if I test positive for a gene mutation?**

We'll develop a comprehensive screening and risk reduction plan. Many options exist, from enhanced screening to preventive surgery.

**Should my family members be tested?**

If you carry a mutation, family members may benefit from testing. We can facilitate referrals for relatives.
