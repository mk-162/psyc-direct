# Why Liver Health Matters

Your liver performs over 500 essential functions — from filtering toxins to processing nutrients, producing proteins, and regulating metabolism. Yet liver problems often develop silently, with few symptoms until significant damage has occurred.

| Liver Function | Why It Matters |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Detoxification** | Processing toxins, medications, and alcohol |
| **Metabolism** | Regulating blood sugar and fat storage |
| **Protein Synthesis** | Producing essential blood proteins |
| **Bile Production** | Essential for digestion and nutrient absorption |
| **Storage** | Vitamins, minerals, and energy reserves |

# Your Questions Answered

**Can liver damage be reversed?**

Early-stage fatty liver and inflammation can often be reversed with lifestyle changes. More advanced damage may be managed but not fully reversed.

**How often should I have my liver checked?**

For most people, annual screening is sufficient. Those with identified issues may need more frequent monitoring.

**Will this detect cirrhosis?**

Blood tests can indicate liver stress, but imaging (ultrasound, FibroScan) may be needed to assess for cirrhosis. We can arrange these if indicated.
