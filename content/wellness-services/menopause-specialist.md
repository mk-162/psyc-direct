# You're Not Alone

Menopause is a natural life transition, but that doesn't mean you have to suffer through it. The hormonal changes that occur during perimenopause and menopause can cause a wide range of symptoms that significantly impact quality of life — work, relationships, sleep, and overall wellbeing.

Yet too often, women's menopausal symptoms are dismissed or minimised. They're told it's "just part of getting older" or offered generic advice that doesn't address their specific situation. Many women suffer in silence, believing there's nothing that can be done.

There is something that can be done. Our Menopause Specialist service provides the understanding, assessment, and treatment options you need to navigate this transition with confidence. We take your symptoms seriously. We investigate thoroughly. And we work with you to find solutions that actually work for your life.

## The Menopause Experience

Every woman's menopause journey is different. Some sail through with minimal symptoms. Others experience a rollercoaster of physical and emotional changes that can last for years. Both experiences are normal — and both deserve support.

The hormonal fluctuations of perimenopause can begin years before your final period, causing symptoms that may come and go unpredictably. Once menopause is reached (defined as 12 months without a period), symptoms may continue or new ones may emerge. And in the postmenopausal years, the lower hormone levels bring new considerations for long-term health.

Understanding where you are in this journey — and what your body is experiencing — is the first step towards feeling better.

# Treatment Options

## Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT)

HRT is the most effective treatment for menopausal symptoms and has been shown to have long-term health benefits for many women. But it's not one-size-fits-all — the type, dose, and delivery method should be tailored to your individual needs.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Oestrogen Only** | For women without a uterus — simplest form of HRT |
| **Combined HRT** | Oestrogen + progesterone for women with a uterus — protects the womb lining |
| **Testosterone** | For low libido and energy — increasingly recognised as important for women too |
| **Bioidentical HRT** | Body-identical hormones — the same molecular structure as your body's own hormones |

## Non-Hormonal Options

Not every woman can or wants to use HRT. We offer a range of non-hormonal approaches that can be effective for many symptoms:

| Approach | When Considered |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Lifestyle Modifications** | Foundation for all treatment plans — nutrition, exercise, stress management, sleep optimisation |
| **Nutritional Support** | Specific nutrients that support menopausal health — phytoestrogens, omega-3s, and more |
| **Herbal Supplements** | Evidence-based botanical options — black cohosh, sage, and others |
| **Prescription Medications** | For specific symptoms — certain antidepressants can help with hot flushes, for example |

# Your Questions Answered

**Is HRT safe?**

For most women, the benefits of HRT outweigh the risks. We conduct a thorough risk assessment and discuss all options with you. The understanding of HRT has evolved significantly — modern HRT, started at the right time and tailored to the individual, is much safer than earlier formulations.

**How long will I need treatment?**

This varies from woman to woman. Many women take HRT for 2-5 years to manage symptoms, though some benefit from longer-term use for bone and cardiovascular protection. We review regularly and adjust based on your needs and preferences.

**Can I see you if I'm already on HRT?**

Absolutely. We can review your current regimen, assess how it's working for you, and optimise as needed. Many women come to us because they're not getting the support they need from their current provider.

**What if I can't take HRT?**

There are many non-hormonal options available, and we'll work with you to find an approach that helps your symptoms. Lifestyle changes, nutritional support, and certain medications can all be effective.
